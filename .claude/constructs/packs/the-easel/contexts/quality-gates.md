# Quality Gates

> Phase gates for the creative workflow. Each gate defines what "done" means before moving to the next phase.

## Gate 1: Grounded

**Trigger**: Before visual exploration begins.

- [ ] Vocabulary atlas exists and has been reviewed
- [ ] At least one vocabulary domain has defined terms
- [ ] Design intent is stated in vocabulary terms (not vague adjectives)

## Gate 2: Explored

**Trigger**: Before capturing results.

- [ ] Generation prompts use vocabulary terms as anchors
- [ ] Multiple directions were explored (not just one)
- [ ] Prompts include both positive and negative terms

## Gate 3: Captured

**Trigger**: Before recording a taste decision.

- [ ] Results are annotated with vocabulary term matches
- [ ] Quality assessment uses defined criteria (not personal preference)
- [ ] Clear distinction between term effectiveness and execution quality

## Gate 4: Recorded

**Trigger**: Before applying design decisions to implementation.

- [ ] TDR follows the template structure
- [ ] Decision references specific vocabulary terms
- [ ] Alternatives are documented with rejection rationale
- [ ] Consequences section addresses future design impact

---

*Quality gates are evaluated by The Easel's skills at each workflow transition.*
